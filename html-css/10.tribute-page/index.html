Alan Turing

The Father of Modern Computing

Here's a time line of Dr. Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born on June 23 in Maida Vale, London.
  • 1931 - Began studying Mathematics at the University of Cambridge.
  • 1936 - Published "On Computable Numbers", introducing the Turing Machine.
  • 1939 - Joined Bletchley Park to break the Enigma code during World War II.
  • 1945 - Made significant contributions to ending the war by deciphering Japanese codes.
  • 1948 - Proposed the Turing Test to assess artificial intelligence.
  • 1952 - Prosecuted for homosexuality, leading to chemical castration.
  • 1954 - Passed away on June 7 at the age of 41.
  • 2013 - Honored with a statue in Manchester and recognized as a war hero.
